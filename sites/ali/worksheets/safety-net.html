Module 3

The Safety Net Worksheet

Build your jealousy protocol and reconnection ritual. Your emotional insurance policy for when things get real.

📢 Part 1: The Code Word

Choose a word or phrase that means "I'm feeling jealous and I need reassurance." Something that doesn't sound dramatic but signals a check-in.

When You Hear the Code Word

  • Pause what you're doing immediately
  • Reassure — genuinely, not defensively
  • Don't say: "You're being ridiculous"
  • Do say: "I hear you. I'm right here. What do you need?"

When You Say the Code Word

  • Identify the specific fear: Am I being replaced? Do I feel left out? Is my ego hurt?
  • Ask for what you need: "Can you text me when you're on your way home?" or "I need 10 minutes of just us."
  • Don't weaponize it: The code word is for real feelings, not for control.

💡 Part 2: Know Your Jealousy Type

Understanding what kind of jealousy you tend to feel helps your partner respond better.

Fear of Replacement

"They're going to leave me for this person."


Fear of Missing Out

"They're having more fun than me."


Ego Jealousy

"I don't like the idea of someone else touching my partner."


💕 Part 3: Our Reconnection Ritual

Define exactly what happens after a date or experience with someone else. This is how you come back to each other.

🚨 Part 4: Emergency Stop

Define when and how you hit pause on the whole arrangement — no shame, no blame.

  • All dates with others are cancelled immediately
  • We schedule a dedicated conversation within 24 hours
  • No decisions are made until both partners feel heard
  • We revisit the Hard Limits Audit and Designer Relationship Worksheet
  • We consider speaking with a therapist if needed

📝 Additional Notes

Remember: The Safety Net isn't about controlling each other. It's about knowing that no matter what happens out there, you have a way back to each other.
