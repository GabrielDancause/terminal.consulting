Travel Safety Checklist

Your go-bag checklist for exploring the lifestyle while travelling. Fill this out before every new destination.

🎒

Essentials — Don't Leave Without

  • Condoms (your own supply) Never rely on anyone else's. Bring more than you think you'll need.
  • Lube (travel-size) Not always easy to find in every country. Bring your preferred brand.
  • Cash in local currency Always have cash. Cards can fail, and some venues are cash-only.
  • Phone fully charged + portable charger Your phone is your lifeline — location sharing, taxi apps, emergency calls.
  • Hotel address written down In case your phone dies. Write it on paper or save it offline.
📱

Phone & Digital Safety

  • Location sharing ON with partner Google Maps, Find My, or WhatsApp live location. Always on.
  • Taxi/ride app installed & working Uber, Grab, Bolt, or whatever works locally. Test it before you need it.
  • Burner phone or secondary SIM Optional, but useful if you want to keep your main number private.
  • Dating apps set up for this location Update your location on Feeld/Tinder. Check what apps are popular locally.
🛡

Safety Protocol

  • Local emergency number saved Not always 911. Look it up before you arrive.
  • Nearest hospital / clinic located Know where to go if something goes wrong. Save the address.
  • Check-in time agreed with partner Set a time for a text or call, especially during solo play.
  • "Come get me" safe word set Different from the jealousy code word. This means "I need out — no questions asked."
  • Exit plan discussed How do you leave a venue quickly? Know your route, have cash for a taxi, keep your phone charged.
🏢

Venue Research

  • Lifestyle clubs researched Google "[city] lifestyle club" or "[city] swinger club." Check reviews and membership requirements.
  • Dress code / entry rules checked Many clubs have strict dress codes or couples-only policies. Don't show up unprepared.
  • Local laws checked Some countries have strict laws around sex work, LGBTQ+ activity, or even public displays of affection. Know before you go.
  • Safe meeting spots identified For first-time meets with new people — always a public place (hotel lobby, cafe, bar).
