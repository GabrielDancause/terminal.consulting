## EMAIL 10 — Pure value, no pitch (Day 25)

**Subject:** Stop calling it "just communicate more"

**Body:**

{{ first_name | default: "Hey" }},

The worst advice I see about relationships — all relationships, not just open ones:

"Just communicate more!"

Yeah, but HOW? What words? What order? When?

Most of us grew up in environments where sex was either never discussed or discussed in ways loaded with shame, fear, or rigid moral framing. Those early messages don't just go away when you become an adult. They become the baseline for how you relate to the topic.

And the research is clear: a meta-analysis from Ohio State University (published in the Journal of Family Psychology) looked at 93 studies and nearly 40,000 people. Couples who communicate openly about sex don't just have better sex — they have stronger relationships. And it's not about how OFTEN you talk about it. It's about the QUALITY of those conversations. Honesty over frequency.

Even if you logically know that sex is natural and healthy, there's often a deeper emotional layer that still flinches at openness. That flinch is learned — and it can be unlearned. But it takes practice.

Start small. You don't have to sit your partner down for a big conversation about your deepest fantasies on day one.

- Comment on something specific after sex: "I really loved when you did that."
- Share an article or video: "This made me think of us."
- Use "I" statements: "I'd really love to try this sometime."

Every time you push through the discomfort, it gets a tiny bit easier.
